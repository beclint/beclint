# Platform Content Guide

## YouTube
### Video Formats
- **Long-form**: 3-8 minutes, cinematic narration, full story arc
- **Shorts**: 15-60 seconds, vertical (9:16), hook-heavy, fast-paced
- **Thumbnails**: 1280x720px, high contrast, dramatic, text overlay

### Best Practices
- Upload at 1080p 30fps minimum
- Use chapters in long-form videos
- Add end screens with subscribe + next video
- First 15 seconds must hook the viewer
- Description: 2-3 paragraphs with story context + links
- Tags: 8-15 relevant tags

### Titles
- Format: "[Story Title] | [Country/Origin] Folktale" or "The [Character] - African Folktale"
- Keep under 60 characters
- Front-load keywords

---

## Instagram
### Content Types
- **Reels**: 15-60 seconds, vertical (9:16), closed captions, trending audio
- **Carousel posts**: 5-10 slides, story breakdown with visuals
- **Stories**: Daily behind-the-scenes, teasers, polls

### Best Practices
- Use 3-5 relevant hashtags in caption, 10+ in first comment
- Post when audience is most active (evenings/weekends)
- Tag location (relevant African country)
- Use Instagram's built-in CC tool for Reels
- Reply to comments within first hour

### Visual Specs
- Reels: 1080x1920px
- Carousel: 1080x1080px (square)
- Stories: 1080x1920px

---

## Facebook
### Content Types
- **Video posts**: Same as YouTube videos (linked or native upload)
- **Image posts**: Story cards with moral lesson text overlay
- **Text posts**: Story teasers, discussion prompts

### Best Practices
- Native uploads perform better than YouTube links
- Post in Facebook Groups related to African culture/storytelling
- Pin a featured story to the top of the page
- Use Facebook's scheduling tool for consistency
- Engage with comments to boost algorithm

### Visual Specs
- Feed images: 1200x630px
- Video: 1280x720px or 1080x1920px (vertical)
