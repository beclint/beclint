# The Woman Who Outwitted the Demon King

**Origin:** Amazigh (Berber) people, Morocco (North Africa)
**Duration:** 4.5 minutes
**Tone:** Mysterious, defiant, empowering

---

## Narration Script

### [0:00 - 0:25] — HOOK
*(Narrator: deep, cinematic, with the sound of a desert wind.)*

**NARRATOR:**
In the High Atlas Mountains of Morocco, where the snow-capped peaks touch the sky and the valleys hide villages that have stood for a thousand years, there is a story about a woman who was smarter than a demon king.

*(Beat. Sound of distant drums.)*

**NARRATOR:**
They say that in the time before the great cities, when the Djinn still walked among us, a girl named **Tala** lived in a village at the foot of Mount Toubkal.

She was not a warrior. She carried no sword. But she carried something far more dangerous: a mind that never stopped working.

### [0:25 - 1:30] — THE DEMON'S DEMAND
*(Narrator: ominous, building tension.)*

**NARRATOR:**
Every year, the Demon King of the mountain would descend upon the villages below. He would appear at the doorway of a different home each time — a towering figure of smoke and shadow, with eyes like burning coals.

And he would demand the same thing.

"Give me your most precious possession," he would say, "or I will burn your village to ash."

The villagers would weep. They would offer him gold, cattle, their finest harvests. But the Demon King would always laugh.

"These are not precious," he would sneer. "Bring me something that truly matters."

And if they could not — the fires would come.

*(Pause. Narrator: quieter.)*

**NARRATOR:**
One year, the Demon King appeared at Tala's door.

### [1:30 - 2:30] — TALA'S CHALLENGE
*(Narrator: Tala's voice should be calm, steady, unafraid.)*

**NARRATOR:**
While her family trembled, Tala stepped forward.

"Great King of the Mountain," she said, her voice steady as the ancient stones, "I will give you what you ask. But first, I must know — what is the most precious thing in the world?"

The Demon King laughed. "You are a child playing games. The most precious thing is power."

Tala tilted her head. "Then why do you not have enough power to find what is precious yourself?"

*(Beat.)*

**NARRATOR:**
The Demon King's eyes narrowed. No one had ever spoken to him this way.

"Clever girl," he growled. "Then tell me — what do you think is the most precious thing?"

Tala smiled.

### [2:30 - 3:30] — THE RIDDLE CONTEST
*(Narrator: playful, rhythmic, like a dance.)*

**NARRATOR:**
"Let us play a game," Tala said. "I will give you three objects. If you can tell me which one is most precious, I will give it to you freely. But if you guess wrong, you must leave our village forever."

The Demon King agreed. He was arrogant. He had never been defeated.

*(Pause.)*

**NARRATOR:**
Tala brought out three objects.

The first was a golden necklace, heavy with rubies — worth more than the entire village.

The second was a clay pot, old and cracked, that had belonged to her grandmother.

The third was a single feather from a mountain eagle, pure white.

*(Narrator: building suspense.)*

**NARRATOR:**
The Demon King studied them. He sniffed the air. He could sense the gold's value. He could sense the pot's history. But the feather... the feather was just a feather.

"The necklace," he declared. "Gold and rubies — the most precious."

Tala shook her head.

### [3:30 - 4:15] — THE REVELATION
*(Narrator: triumphant, warm.)*

**NARRATOR:**
"The necklace is just metal and stone, mined from your own mountain," she said. "The pot is memory — but memories fade. The feather, however..."

She held it up. The wind caught it, and it danced.

**NARRATOR:**
"...the feather is freedom. It belongs to no one. It answers to no one. It is precious because it cannot be owned."

*(Beat.)*

**NARRATOR:**
The Demon King roared in fury. But a promise was a promise, even for a demon. He dissolved into smoke and retreated to his mountain peak.

The villagers never saw him again.

### [4:15 - 4:30] — MORAL
*(Narrator: slow, thoughtful, teaching.)*

**NARRATOR:**
The Amazigh say: *"Win yebɣan ad iẓer, yella d ameẓẓuɣ"* — "The one who seeks to know must first learn to see."

Tala taught her people that the most valuable things in life cannot be bought, traded, or stolen. They can only be understood.

And sometimes, a single feather is worth more than all the gold in the mountain.

## Narration Notes
- **The Demon King's voice:** Deep, echoing, slightly distorted. A voice of ancient power.
- **Tala's voice:** Clear, calm, with a hint of a smile. She is never afraid.
- **The riddle scene:** Playful but building tension. Like a chess match.
- **The feather reveal:** Pause before "the feather is freedom." Let the word "freedom" hang in the air.
- **Closing:** Soft, like a grandmother teaching a grandchild.
