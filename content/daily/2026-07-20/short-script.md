# The Twins and the Crocodile's Secret — Short Form

## Script

### [0:00 - 0:03] — HOOK
*(Text on screen: "A CROCODILE GAVE THEM A TEST")*

**NARRATOR:**
An ancient crocodile offered two brothers a secret treasure. Only one could earn it.

### [0:03 - 0:12]
*(Visual: Two boys standing before a massive crocodile rising from the water.)*

**NARRATOR:**
The Maasai of East Africa tell the story of twins — Leshao, who was proud and impatient, and Naipon, who was quiet and wise.

### [0:12 - 0:25]
*(Visual: Both boys carrying calabashes of water. One running, splashing. One walking slowly.)*

**NARRATOR:**
The crocodile told them to carry water home without spilling a drop. Leshao ran — and lost everything. Naipon walked slowly, accepting the small losses.

### [0:25 - 0:38]
*(Visual: Naipon's calabash with a small hole, water dripping steadily.)*

**NARRATOR:**
Naipon had pierced a small hole in his gourd. The water dripped, but he didn't panic. He kept walking. Steady. Patient.

### [0:38 - 0:48]
*(Visual: The crocodile whispering to Naipon, the sun setting behind them.)*

**NARRATOR:**
The crocodile whispered the secret of the lake to Naipon. Leshao demanded to know it too. But some secrets can only be earned — never given.

### [0:48 - 0:50] — MORAL
*(Text overlay: "PATIENCE IS THE MOTHER OF ALL VIRTUES")*

**NARRATOR:**
The Maasai say: "Patience is the mother of all virtues." Rushing through life spills everything worth keeping.
